# Progression Rules

## Double progression

Most exercises use a rep range.

Example:

```text
4 sets x 10-15 reps
```

In Strong, the template uses the minimum target:

```text
4 sets x 10 reps
```

The Sticky Note tells the real target:

```text
Meta: 10-15 reps
```

## When to increase load

Increase load when all working sets reach the top of the rep range with good technique.

Examples:

```text
15/15/15/15 -> increase load
12/12/12 -> increase load when target is 8-12
20/20/20 -> increase load when target is 12-20
```

## RIR

RIR means repetitions in reserve.

RIR 2 means stopping when you feel you could still perform about 2 more reps.

Current rule:

- Most exercises: stop around RIR 2.
- Last set may reach RIR 1.
- Avoid true failure on movements that may stress lumbar spine or shoulder.

## Load increments

Suggested increments:

- Large machines: +2.5 to +5 kg
- Smaller machines/cables: +1 to +2.5 kg
- Dumbbells: smallest available increment

## When to reduce load

Reduce load if:

- technique breaks down
- lumbar discomfort appears
- shoulder pain appears
- movement becomes unstable
- breathing/bracing fails

## Goal

The goal is not to prove strength in one workout.

The goal is to accumulate high-quality training weeks while preserving muscle during fat loss.
